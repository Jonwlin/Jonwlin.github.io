<Aside>
**Always redeem the smallest cash-off option** — $5 off at Soft Swerve (50 pts, $0.10/pt) and $1 off at HeyTea (100 pts, $0.01/pt) for solo orders. The one exception: HeyTea's 15% off (300 pts) becomes the best option once your order clears $20, maxing out at $5 off a $33.33+ order ($0.017/pt). The premium-sounding rewards — "free pints," "free drink" — are traps worth half the value or worse.
</Aside>

I wanted the optimal redemption strategy for loyalty points at two spots I hit constantly — Soft Swerve (ice cream) and HeyTea (boba/tea) — instead of blindly saving up for the big-ticket rewards. So I priced out every option by the only metric that matters: **dollars saved per point**.

## How I valued each reward

The math is just `value of the reward ÷ points it costs` = $/point. Higher is better. Prices I used:

- **Soft Swerve:** $7.50 for a 1-scoop cup, $2 per topping, $2 waffle-cone upgrade, $12.50 per pint.
- **HeyTea:** $8 average per drink.

The headline finding: the cheapest, most boring reward (a few dollars off) almost always wins, and the aspirational rewards you'd save months for are the worst per-point deals.

## Soft Swerve

| Option | Points | Value | $/Point |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ⭐ $5 off | 50 | $5.00 | $0.100 |
| Waffle cone upgrade | 25 | $2.00 | $0.080 |
| Cup/cone + 1 topping | 120 | $9.50 | $0.079 |
| Two pints | 500 | $25.00 | $0.050 |
| Sundae cup | 200 | ? | ? |

`$5 off` dominates everything by 25%+. The "two pints" reward — the one that *feels* like the jackpot — is the worst confirmed option at half the value of just taking $5 off.

## HeyTea

| Option | Points | Value | $/Point |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ⭐ 15% off – 3+ drinks ($24+) | 300 | $3.60–$5.00 | $0.012–$0.017 |
| ⭐ $1 off (solo) | 100 | $1.00 | $0.0100 |
| 15% off – 2 drinks ($16) | 300 | $2.40 | $0.0080 |
| 50% off 2nd cup | 500 | $4.00 | $0.0080 |
| Buy 1 get 1 free | 1000 | $8.00 | $0.0080 |
| 50% off single drink | 700 | $4.00 | $0.0057 |
| Free single drink | 1500 | $8.00 | $0.0053 |
| 15% off – 1 drink ($8) | 300 | $1.20 | $0.0040 |

Solo, take `$1 off` — it's the highest $/point you can reliably hit. Ordering for a group flips the answer to `15% off`.

> The "free drink" (1500 pts) is strictly worse than "buy 1 get 1 free" (1000 pts): same $8 of value, but 500 extra points. Never save for it.

## The 15%-off breakeven

15% off only beats `$1 off` once the discount is worth more than ~$3, i.e. `15% × order > $3.00` → **order > $20**. Below that, the flat $1 off wins; above it, the percentage scales with your group:

- 1 drink ($8): saves $1.20 → $0.004/pt (worst)
- 2 drinks ($16): saves $2.40 → $0.008/pt (ties BOGO and 50%-off-2nd)
- 3+ drinks ($24+): saves $3.60–$5.00 → $0.012–$0.017/pt (best)

It caps at $5 of savings on a $33.33 order, so there's no benefit to a single giant order beyond that.

## Caveats

- All HeyTea math assumes $8/drink — pricier specialty drinks push the 15%-off breakeven _lower_.
- Soft Swerve's sundae cup (200 pts) is unranked: no pricing available. It'd only compete if priced above ~$20, which is unlikely.
- 15% off needs 3+ drinks ($24+) to beat $1 off — if you rarely order for a group, just take $1 off.
- BOGO (1000 pts) and 50%-off-2nd-cup (500 pts) are equal per point, but the 500-pt option accumulates twice as fast.
